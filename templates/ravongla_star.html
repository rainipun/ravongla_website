Ganesh Dham

The room is named after a natural Dham of Sokpey village. A natural lord Ganesh statue was discovered by locals especially cattle rarers who keep on moving for green pastures. After it was discovered the local people gathered and payed their obeisance to Lord Ganesh in the form of Yagnas (Holy fires). It is believed by the locals that after the discovery of the Statue the landslide of the village decreased considerably, they consider it as a blessing from the Lord.

KIRATESHWAR DHAM

This room is named after a holy Dham called Kirateshwar Dham located 25kms away from the hotel towards the west direction. This is actually a Shiva temple where lord Shiva had taken the form of Kirateshwar during the spiritual tour to Van Vicharan. This spot is located in South Sikkim on the banks of the river Rangit on the way to Gyalshing-Pelling. The Kirateshwar temple geographically lies in Hingdam village of South Sikkim which touches the border of West district.

BORONG TSA CHU

The Super Deluxe Double Room, located on the 3rd floor and facing west, offers stunning views of snow-capped mountains. Covering 290 sq ft, it features an entrance to the west, a washroom in the South-Eastern corner, and a small sitting area. Named after Borong's Tsa Chu hot spring, it mirrors the spring's natural warmth and comfort. This room’s design and ambiance are inspired by the accessibility and spaciousness of the Tsa Chu hot spring, which is known for its natural sulphurated waters and scenic surroundings.

KHANDOSANGPHU CAVE

The Super Deluxe Semi Suite Room, located on the 2nd floor and facing east, spans 290 sq ft. It features a small seating area with an inbuilt fireplace, a decorated wardrobe with a jewelry safe, and a washroom in the north-western corner. Entering from the south, the room's ambiance is inspired by Khando-sangphu, a sacred cave south of Tashiding, known for its meditative peace and spiritual significance. This room aims to provide a similar sense of tranquility and comfort.

SARCHOGBEPHU CAVE

The Super Deluxe Room, located on the 2nd floor facing the north-west, covers 200 sq ft. It features a natural fireplace in the north-western corner and a washroom in the north-eastern corner. Named after the "Cave of the Eastern Direction," a vast, sacred cave near Tashiding, the room reflects the cave’s spiritual ambiance and mystic significance. The cave, extending towards Mt. Tendong and Mt. Maenam, is renowned for its religious patterns and hidden treasures, and is accessible from Ravongla with a 20-minute walk.

LHARINYINGPHU CAVE

This super deluxe honeymoon suite, located in the south-western direction, features a southern sitting area and a washroom in the south-east corner, covering approximately 290 sq ft. A fireplace in the south-west corner adds to its secluded charm. Named "Heart of the Gods Cave," it reflects the sacredness of the northern cave, known for its spiritual significance and hidden relics. Accessible via a medium to hard three-day trek from Labdang village, the route offers scenic views of Tathagatha Tsal, Ralong Monastery, and other beautiful sites.

MAHADEV CAVE

This super deluxe suite, spanning 360 sq ft and located in the eastern direction, features a divided layout with a sitting area and bedroom separated by a half wall. It has a fireplace in the south-western corner and is entered from the north-east. The washroom is in the north-eastern corner, with a wardrobe on the eastern wall and concrete slabs in the south-eastern corner. A small eastern window lets in morning sunlight. Named after a Mahadevsthan cave near Lingee village, associated with Lord Shiva and Vedic rituals, the suite reflects spiritual significance and offers views of Ramba waterfalls and local village life.

POTHI DHUNGA

This standard room, located on the western side of the eco-friendly house, offers views of the snow-capped Mt. Kanchenjunga range. Positioned centrally on the first floor, it has a common sitting area to the east and is accessed from the east. The washroom is in the south-west corner, and the room covers about 190 sq ft. Named “Pothi Dhunga” (Hen-shaped Rock), it references a peak near Ravongla, known for its trekking trails and folklore about warning locals of natural disasters. On clear mornings, powerful binoculars reveal the plains of North Bengal.
